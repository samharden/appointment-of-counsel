What Happens in DUI Cases in Judge Conrad's Courtroom


In Judge Conrad's courtroom 52% of people accused of a DUI are convicted. 37% have their case reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving, and only 11% of DUI cases are dismissed.

Do people hire an attorney for DUI cases in Judge Conrad's courtroom?

Nearly everyone in Judge Conrad's courtroom who is accused of DUI has an attorney for their case. The majority of people (69%) hire a private attorney, while less than a quarter (16%) use the public defender. Only 15% of people do not hire an attorney, which is called "Pro Se."

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is a big advantage in DUI cases in Judge Conrad's courtroom. People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction 56% of the time, while people who had the public defender avoid a DUI conviction 43% of the time. People who do not have an attorney ("Pro Se") avoid a DUI conviction only 17% of the time.

The average for judges in Hillsborough County is slightly higher for private attorneys, and slightly lower for those represented by the public defender. People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction 51% of the time, while people who use the public defender only avoid a DUI conviction 40% of the time. People who go without an attorney avoid a DUI conviction only 26% of the time.

Why is avoiding a DUI conviction so important?

DUI convictions not only carry stiff penalties, they can have lasting economic consequences for both your insurance and employment. If you are convicted of a DUI, your car insurance rates will increase and can add up to thousands of dollars over time. If you hold a professional license a DUI conviction can put your ability to work in jeopardy.

Can I get my DUI case dismissed or dropped?

Only 10% of DUI cases are dismissed or dropped in Judge Conrad's courtroom.  People who hire a private attorney have their case dropped or dismissed only 10% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case dropped or dismissed 22% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") get their case dismissed or dropped 6% of the time.

What about DUI cases reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving?

37% of DUI cases in Judge Conrad's courtroom are reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving. People who hire a private attorney have their case reduced to Reckless Driving  46% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case reduced only 21% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") get their case reduced to Reckless Driving just 10% of the time.

Shouldn't I just represent myself?

15% of people accused of DUI represent themselves in Judge Conrad's courtroom. These people, on average, are far more likely to receive a DUI conviction than people who hire private attorneys or are represented by a Public Defender.
