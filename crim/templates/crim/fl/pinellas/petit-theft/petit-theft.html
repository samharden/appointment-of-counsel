Petit Theft Cases in Pinellas County

60% of people charged with Petit Theft are convicted. 15% of cases are dismissed, and 25% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record.

Do people hire an attorney for Petit Theft cases in Pinellas County?

13% of people charged with Petit Theft hire a private attorney in Pinellas County. 54% go with the Public Defender, while 33% decide to handle their cases on their own without an attorney.

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is a clear advantage over the public defender in these cases in Pinellas County. People who hired a private attorney were convicted just 35% of the time, while people who had the public defender were convicted 78% of the time. People without an attorney, also known as "Pro Se," were convicted 39% of the time.

Can I get my Petit Theft case dismissed or dropped?

People who hire a private attorney have their cases dismissed 31% of the time, while people who use the public defender have their cases dismissed 7% of the time. People without an attorney have their case dismissed 23% of the time.

What about getting a Withhold of Adjudication?

People who hired a private attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 34% of the time, while people with the public defender receive a withhold of adjudication 15% of the time. People without an attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 38% of the time.

Does what judge I have make a difference?

People with cases in front of Judge Pierce are convicted of Petit Theft 91% of the time, while people with cases in front of Judge Carballo are convicted 52% of the time.

People with cases in front of Judges Carballo and Horrox have their cases dismissed 19% of the time, while 0% of people with a Petit Theft case in Judge Pierce's or Overton's courtrooms have their cases dismissed.

People with cases in front of Judges Bedinghaus and Carballo receive a withhold of adjudication 29% of the time, while just 9% of people with cases in front of Judge Pierce received a withhold of adjudication over the past five years.

What is the typical penalty for Petit Theft?

The most common penalty for Petit Theft is 6 months probation and a fine in the amount of $200-300.
