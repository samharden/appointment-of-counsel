Battery Cases in Judge Levine's Courtroom

48% of people charged with Battery are convicted. 33% of cases are dismissed, and 19% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record.

Do people hire an attorney for Battery cases in Levine's courtroom?

22% of people charged with Battery hire a private attorney in Judge Levine's courtroom. 59% go with the Public Defender, while 19% decide to handle their cases on their own without an attorney.

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is a clear advantage over the public defender in these cases in Levine's courtroom. People who hire a private attorney are convicted just 29% of the time, while people who have the public defender are convicted 63% of the time. People without an attorney, also known as "Pro Se," are convicted 27% of the time.

Across Pinellas County people who hire a private attorney are convicted of Battery 49% of the time, while people represented by the public defender are convicted 69% of the time. People who handle their cases on their own, also known as "Pro Se", are convicted 18% of the time.

Can I get my Battery case dismissed or dropped?

People who hire a private attorney have their cases dismissed 41% of the time, while people who use the public defender have their cases dismissed 25% of the time. People without an attorney have their case dismissed 48% of the time.

What about getting a Withhold of Adjudication?

People who hire a private attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 29% of the time, while people with the public defender receive a withhold of adjudication 13% of the time. People without an attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 24% of the time.

What is the typical penalty for Battery?

The most common penalty for Battery is 6 months probation and a fine in the amount of $600-700.
