Battery Cases in Pinellas County

55% of people charged with Battery are convicted. 31% of cases are dismissed, and 14% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record.

Do people hire an attorney for Battery cases in Pinellas County?

18% of people charged with Battery hire a private attorney in Pinellas County. 64% go with the Public Defender, while 18% decide to handle their cases on their own without an attorney.

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is a clear advantage over the public defender in these cases in Pinellas County. People who hired a private attorney were convicted just 40% of the time, while people who had the public defender were convicted 69% of the time. People without an attorney, also known as "Pro Se," were convicted 18% of the time.

Can I get my Battery case dismissed or dropped?

People who hire a private attorney have their cases dismissed 37% of the time, while people who use the public defender have their cases dismissed 20% of the time. People without an attorney have their case dismissed 65% of the time.

What about getting a Withhold of Adjudication?

People who hired a private attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 23% of the time, while people with the public defender receive a withhold of adjudication 10% of the time. People without an attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 17% of the time.

Does what judge I have make a difference?

People with cases in front of Judge McKyton are convicted of Battery 68% of the time, while people with cases in front of Judge Pierce are convicted only 33% of the time.

People with cases in front of Judge McKyton have their cases dismissed 15% of the time, while 67% of people with a Battery case in Judge Pierce's courtroom have their cases dismissed.

People with cases in front of Judge Dittmer receive a withhold of adjudication 24% of the time, while no one with cases in front of Judge Pierce received a withhold of adjudication over the past five years.

What is the typical penalty for Battery?

The most common penalty for Battery is 6 months probation and a fine in the amount of $600-700.
