Driving While License Suspended Cases in Pinellas County

52% of people charged with Driving While License Suspended are convicted. 21% of cases are dismissed, and 27% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record.

Do people hire an attorney for Driving While License Suspended cases in Pinellas County?

8% of people charged with Driving While License Suspended hire a private attorney in Pinellas County. 30% go with the Public Defender, while 62% decide to handle their cases on their own without an attorney.

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is a clear advantage over the public defender in these cases in Pinellas County. People who hired a private attorney were convicted just 62% of the time, while people who had the public defender were convicted 88% of the time. People without an attorney, also known as "Pro Se," were convicted 33% of the time.

Can I get my Driving While License Suspended case dismissed or dropped?

People who hire a private attorney have their cases dismissed 11% of the time, while people who use the public defender have their cases dismissed 3% of the time. People without an attorney have their case dismissed 32% of the time.

What about getting a Withhold of Adjudication?

People who hired a private attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 27% of the time, while people with the public defender receive a withhold of adjudication 9% of the time. People without an attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 35% of the time.

Does what judge I have make a difference?

People with cases in front of Judges Horrox and McKyton are convicted of Driving While License Suspended 90% of the time, while people with cases in front of Judge Pierce are convicted only 38% of the time.

People with cases in front of Judge McKyton have their cases dismissed 2% of the time, while 29% of people with a Driving While License Suspended case in Judge Overton's courtroom have their cases dismissed.

People with cases in front of Judge Horrox receive a withhold of adjudication 6% of the time, while 58% with cases in front of Judge Pierce receive a withhold of adjudication.

What is the typical penalty for Driving While License Suspended?

The most common penalty for Driving While License Suspended is a $270 fine. Less than 1% of people receive probation for this charge.
