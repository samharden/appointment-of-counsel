Driving While License Suspended Cases in Judge Lefler's Courtroom

Know Your Case, Know Your Chances.

45% of people charged with Driving While License Suspended With Knowledge are convicted. 30% of cases are dismissed, and 25% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record.

Do people hire an attorney for Driving While License Suspended cases in Judge Lefler's courtroom?

Only 16% of people charged with Driving While License Suspended With Knowledge hire a private attorney in Judge Lefler's courtroom. 16% go with the Public Defender. 69% decide to handle their cases on their own, which is called "Pro Se."

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is a slight advantage in Judge Lefler's courtroom. People who hire a private attorney are convicted 44% of the time, while people who have the public defender are convicted 65% of the time. People without an attorney, also known as "Pro Se," are convicted 41% of the time.

Across Hillsborough County people who hired a private attorney are convicted of Driving While License Suspended With Knowledge 43% of the time, while people represented by the public defender are convicted 69% of the time. People who handle their cases on their own, also known as "Pro Se", are convicted 46% of the time.

Can I get my Driving While License Suspended case dismissed or dropped?

People who hire a private attorney have their cases dismissed 37% of the time, while people who have the public defender have their cases dismissed 22% of the time. People without an attorney, also known as "Pro Se," have their case dismissed 30% of the time.

What about getting a Withhold of Adjudication?

People who hire a private attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 19% of the time, while people with the public defender receive a withhold of adjudication 13% of the time. People without an attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 29% of the time.

What is the typical penalty for Driving While License Suspended?

The most common penalty for Driving While License Suspended with Knowledge is a $270 fine. Less than 1% of people receive probation for this charge.
