DUI Cases in Judge Greco's Courtroom

Know Your Case, Know Your Chances.

In Judge Greco's courtroom 56% of people accused of a DUI are convicted. 33% have their case reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving, and only 10% of DUI cases are dismissed.

Do people hire an attorney for DUI cases in Judge Greco's courtroom?

_Nearly everyone in Judge Greco's courtroom who is accused of DUI has an attorney for their case. The majority of people (68%) hire a private attorney, while less than a quarter (15%) use the public defender. Only 17% of people do not hire an attorney, which is called "Pro Se."

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is a big advantage in DUI cases in Judge Greco's courtroom. People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction 50% of the time, while people who had the public defender avoid a DUI conviction 45% of the time. People who do not have an attorney ("Pro Se") avoid a DUI conviction only 17% of the time.

The average for judges in Hillsborough County is slightly higher for private attorneys, and slightly lower for those represented by the public defender. People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction 51% of the time, while people who use the public defender only avoid a DUI conviction 40% of the time. People who go without an attorney avoid a DUI conviction only 26% of the time.

Why is avoiding a DUI conviction so important?

DUI convictions not only carry stiff penalties, they can have lasting economic consequences for both your insurance and employment. If you are convicted of a DUI, your car insurance rates will increase and can add up to thousands of dollars over time. If you hold a professional license a DUI conviction can put your ability to work in jeopardy.

Can I get my DUI case dismissed or dropped?

Only 10% of DUI cases are dismissed or dropped in Judge Greco's courtroom. People who hire a private attorney have their case dropped or dismissed only 9% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case dropped or dismissed 22% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") get their case dismissed or dropped 7% of the time.

What about DUI cases reduced to thelesser charge of Reckless Driving?

33% of DUI cases in Judge Greco's courtroom are reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving. People who hire a private attorney have their case reduced to Reckless Driving 41% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case reduced only 23% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") get their case reduced to Reckless Driving just 10% of the time.

Shouldn't I just represent myself?

7% of people accused of DUI represent themselves in Judge Greco's courtroom. These people, on average, are far more likely to receive a DUI conviction than people who hire private attorneys or are represented by a Public Defender.
