DUI Cases in Hillsborough County

Know Your Case, Know Your Chances.

Over half of DUI cases (54%) result in a DUI conviction. 34% of DUI cases are reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving. Only 12% of people avoid a DUI conviction by having their case dismissed.

Do people hire an attorney for DUI cases in Hillsborough County?

Yes. More people hire an attorney for a DUI charge than for any other misdemeanor traffic crime in Hillsborough county. 68% of people charged with a DUI hire a private attorney. 14% are represented by the Public Defender. 18% decide to handle their cases on their own without an attorney, which is called "Pro Se."

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Yes. People who hire a private attorney avoid a DUI conviction more than half of the time (51%). People who had the public defender avoid a DUI conviction 40% of the time. People who do not have an attorney and represent themselves ("Pro Se") only avoid a DUI conviction 26% of the time.

People who hire a private attorney are convicted of DUI in less than half of cases (49%). People who use the public defender are convicted of DUI 60% of the time. People who go without an attorney and represent themselves ("Pro Se") are convicted of DUI 73% of the time.

Why is avoiding a DUI conviction so important?

DUI convictions not only carry stiff penalties, they can have lasting economic consequences for both your insurance and employment. If you are convicted of a DUI, your car insurance rates will increase and can add up to thousands of dollars over time. If you hold a professional license a DUI conviction can put your ability to work in jeopardy.

Does what judge I have make a difference?

The average rate of conviction for DUI cases across all judges is 54%. People with private attorneys are convicted less often than average in most judge's courtrooms.

People with the public defender are convicted more often than the average in most judge’s courtrooms, with the exception of Judges McNeil and Myers.

People who go without an attorney and represent themselves are convicted more often than the average in every judge’s courtroom, with the exception of Judge Myers.
