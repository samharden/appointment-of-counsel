DUI Cases in Judge Farr's Courtroom

Know Your Case, Know Your Chances.

In Judge Farr's courtroom 61% of people accused of a DUI are convicted. 32% have their case reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving, and only 7% of DUI cases are dismissed or dropped.

Do people hire an attorney for DUI cases in Judge Farr's courtroom?

Nearly everyone in Judge Farr's courtroom who is accused of DUI has an attorney for their case. The majority of people (64%) hire a private attorney, while slightly more than a quarter (28%) use the public defender. Only 8% of people do not hire an attorney, which is what the court system calls "Pro Se."

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is a big advantage in DUI cases in Judge Farr's courtroom. People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction, either through having their case dismissed or reduced to reckless driving, 46% of the time, while people who had the public defender only avoided a DUI conviction 26% of the time. People who went Pro Se avoided a DUI conviction 27% of the time.

The average for judges in Hillsborough County is slightly higher for private attorneys, and slightly lower for those represented by the public defender. People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction 51% of the time, while people who use the public defender only avoid a DUI conviction 40% of the time. People who go without an attorney avoid a DUI conviction only 26% of the time.

Why is avoiding a DUI conviction so important?

DUI convictions not only carry stiff penalties, they can have lasting economic consequences for both your insurance and employment. If you are convicted of a DUI, your car insurance rates will increase and can add up to thousands of dollars over time. If you hold a professional license a DUI conviction can put your ability to work in jeopardy.

Can I get my DUI case dismissed or dropped?

Only 7% of DUI cases in Judge Farr's courtroom are dismissed or dropped in Judge Farr's courtroom.
People who hired a private attorney have their case dropped or dismissed only 6% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case dismissed or dropped 10% of the time, and people who went Pro Se have their case dismissed or dropped 6% of the time.

What about DUI cases reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving?

32% of DUI cases in Judge Farr's courtroom are reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving.
People who hired a private attorney have their case reduced to Reckless Driving 40% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case reduced only 17% of the time. People who went Pro Se have their case reduced to Reckless Driving 21% of the time.

Shouldn't I just represent myself?

Over the past five years only 52 people represented themselves in a DUI case in Judge Farr's courtroom. While they avoided a DUI conviction at a nearly the same rate than those represented by the public defender, such a small sample size does not have much meaning as an overall trend, especially when Judge Farr was only recently appointed to the criminal traffic bench.
