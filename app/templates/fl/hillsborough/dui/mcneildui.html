DUI Cases in Judge McNeil's Courtroom

Know Your Case, Know Your Chances.

In Judge McNeil's courtroom 59% of people accused of a DUI are convicted. 28% have their case reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving, and only 13% of DUI cases are dismissed.

Do people hire an attorney for DUI cases in Judge McNeil's courtroom?

Almost 70% of people in Judge McNeil's courtroom who are accused of DUI have an attorney for their case. The majority of people (64%) hire a private attorney, while only 5% use the public defender. 31% of people do not hire an attorney.

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction 45% of the time, while people who have the public defender avoid a DUI conviction 55% of the time. People who do not have an attorney ("Pro Se") avoid a DUI conviction only 33% of the time.

The average for judges in Hillsborough County is higher for private attorneys and lower for those represented by the public defender. People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction 51% of the time, while people who had the public defender only avoid a DUI conviction 40% of the time. People who go without an attorney avoid a DUI conviction only 26% of the time.

Why is avoiding a DUI conviction so important?

DUI convictions not only carry stiff penalties, they can have lasting economic consequences for both your insurance and employment. If you are convicted of a DUI, your car insurance rates will increase and can add up to thousands of dollars over time. If you hold a professional license a DUI conviction can put your ability to work in jeopardy.

Can I get my DUI case dismissed or dropped?

Only 13% of the total DUI cases in Judge McNeil's courtroom are dismissed. People who hire a private attorney have their case dropped or dismissed only 11% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have 31% of their cases dropped, the highest among judges in Hillsborough County. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") get their case dismissed or dropped 16% of the time.

What about DUI cases reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving?

28% of the total DUI cases are reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving in Judge McNeil's courtroom. People who hire a private attorney have their case reduced to Reckless Driving 34% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case reduced only 24% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") get their case reduced to Reckless Driving 17% of the time.

Shouldn't I just represent myself?

People without an attorney, on average, are far more likely to receive a DUI conviction than people who hired private attorneys or used the public defender in Judge McNeil's courtroom. People who go without an attorney are convicted of a DUI charge 67% of the time, while those with the public defender are convicted only 45% of the time. People who hire a private attorney are convicted 55% of the time.
